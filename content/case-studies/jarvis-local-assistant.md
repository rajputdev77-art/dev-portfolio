## Context

I wanted my own assistant. Not a subscription to someone else's. Something that runs locally, respects my data, and does what I actually need it to do.

## The Problem

Every assistant in the market is a black box — you send data to their servers, you get a canned response back, and you hope it is useful. Building one from scratch meant understanding every layer: speech-to-text, model inference, tool use, text-to-speech, orchestration.

## How I Approached It

Broke the problem into layers. Got each layer working independently before connecting them. Prioritized "it works on my machine today" over "it works perfectly in theory."
